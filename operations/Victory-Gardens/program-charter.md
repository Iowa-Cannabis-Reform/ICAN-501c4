# Victory Gardens Program – Charter  
Iowa Cannabis Action Network (ICAN)

## Program Purpose
The Victory Gardens Program empowers Iowa residents, families, farmers, and communities to:
- Grow food and herbal plants using regenerative practices  
- Contribute surplus produce to local food banks  
- Build local community engagement around sustainable living  
- Promote public education about cannabis, hemp, herbal medicine, and soil health  
- Create a statewide grassroots network for future advocacy work

Victory Gardens operates as a **nonpartisan, community-education program** under ICAN’s 501(c)(4) mission.

---

## Program Goals
1. **Expand local food security** through garden-grown donations.  
2. **Teach regenerative agriculture**, soil health, composting, and terroir.  
3. **Connect communities** through gardening, education, and volunteerism.  
4. **Grow a statewide garden map**, showcasing participants and community partners.  
5. **Build public understanding** of plant-based wellness and sustainable living.  
6. **Develop grassroots capacity** to support cannabis policy reform through education.

---

## Guiding Principles
- Accessibility: Anyone with a container, yard, or farm can participate.  
- Sustainability: Promote soil health, biodiversity, and regenerative practices.  
- Community: Encourage collaboration, mutual aid, and local food donation.  
- Education: Teach accurate, science-based botanical knowledge.  
- Integrity: Maintain C4 compliance — no partisan or candidate actions.

---

## Governance
The Victory Gardens Program is operated by **ICAN’s Program Operations Department** and must comply with:
- ICAN bylaws  
- ICAN’s Conflict of Interest Policy  
- C4 communication and advocacy rules  
- All safety and non-discrimination requirements  

---

## Deliverables
- Public-facing Garden Map  
- Garden Registration System  
- Education Resource Library  
- Community Event Calendar  
- Annual “State of the Gardens” Report
