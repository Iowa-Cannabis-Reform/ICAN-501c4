# ICAN Volunteer Handbook  
Iowa Cannabis Action Network (501(c)(4))

Welcome to the Iowa Cannabis Action Network (ICAN).  
Our volunteers form the foundation of our work in public education, community outreach, and grassroots organizing around cannabis reform and regenerative living.

This handbook explains our mission, expectations, safety rules, and volunteer rights.

---

## 1. Who We Are
ICAN is a 501(c)(4) social welfare organization dedicated to:
- Cannabis education  
- Advocacy for civil liberties and patient rights  
- Regenerative community programs (such as Victory Gardens)  
- Public engagement  
- Building a statewide network for policy reform  

---

## 2. Volunteer Mission
Volunteers help ICAN:
- Educate communities  
- Run the Victory Gardens Program  
- Staff events  
- Distribute educational materials  
- Support public research and storytelling  
- Engage Iowans in issue-based policy reform  

Volunteers are **not** permitted to engage in PAC or electoral activity through ICAN.

---

## 3. Volunteer Rights
Volunteers have the right to:
- A safe and welcoming environment  
- Clear expectations  
- Training and support  
- Respect for their time  
- No discrimination or harassment  
- End participation at any time

## 5. Non-Discrimination Policy
ICAN does not discriminate on the basis of:
- Race  
- Color  
- Religion  
- Gender or identity  
- Sexual orientation  
- National origin  
- Disability  
- Veteran status  

---

## 6. Safety Rules
- Follow on-site directions during events  
- Be aware of tools, hazards, and weather conditions  
- Do not enter private areas without permission  
- Use signage provided for Victory Gardens  
- Report hazards immediately  

---

## 7. Confidentiality
Volunteers may have access to:
- Contact information  
- Survey or story submissions  

This information must be kept confidential.

---

## 8. C4 Compliance
Volunteers must NOT:
- Endorse candidates while representing ICAN  
- Engage in electioneering  
- Discuss PAC operations  
- Release internal strategies  

Volunteers MAY:
- Educate the public  
- Support issue advocacy  
- Participate in nonpartisan events  

---

## 9. Ending Volunteer Service
Help us keep accurate records by notifying ICAN if:
- You wish to pause or end volunteering  
- Your contact information changes  
- You want to shift to a different role  

Thank you for helping build a healthier, more empowered Iowa.
